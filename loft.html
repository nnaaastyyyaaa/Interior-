Loft style

The Industrial style originated in the 1940s in New York, when abandoned industrial buildings were converted into residential spaces. These large open areas became popular among artists and creative professionals.

Loft interiors are characterized by high ceilings, large windows, exposed brick walls, and visible construction elements such as pipes and beams. One of the key features of this style is the use of raw materials and an open-plan layout.

This style is perfect for people who appreciate open-plan spaces and authentic Loft architecture with raw architectural details.

Materials commonly used

  • Brick
  • Concrete
  • Metal
  • Glass

Advantages of loft

  • Spacious feeling

    Loft interiors create a strong sense of openness because they usually avoid unnecessary walls and partitions. High ceilings and large windows make the space feel much bigger than it actually is.

  • Large amount of natural light

    One of the main advantages of Loft design is the use of oversized windows. Natural light fills the room during the day, reducing the need for artificial lighting and creating a bright atmosphere.

  • Modern and artistic atmosphere

    The combination of brick walls, metal details, and open layouts gives the interior a creative and contemporary look. This style is often associated with artistic studios and modern urban living.

  • Flexibility in layout

    Since Loft spaces are typically open-plan, furniture can be arranged in different ways depending on personal needs. This makes the interior adaptable and functional.

  • Unique architectural character

    Exposed beams, pipes, and concrete surfaces add individuality to the space. Instead of hiding structural elements, Loft style turns them into decorative features.

  • Minimal decoration required

    Because the architecture itself becomes part of the design, there is less need for excessive decoration. The raw materials create visual interest on their own.

Definitions

Open space
A layout design without internal walls dividing the main living areas, creating a continuous and flexible space.
Loft
An open residential space originally converted from an industrial building, typically featuring high ceilings and exposed structural elements.
